About Us

At African Network for Change, A society without conflict but love where women, men, youth and children, and other vulnerable groups are protected, cared for, and nurtured to be self-reliant.

Who We Are

Our Story

African Network for Change is an indigenous (NGO) operating in Northern Uganda in partnership with Doctors with Africa (CUAMM), University of Bari- Department of Infectious diseases, University of Sacred Heart in Rome and Italian Cooperation with the purpose to promote HIV/AIDS-Tuberculosis Prevention; Rise against Sexual and Gender-Based Violence; Supporting Adolescent Mothers, Promoting Maternal and Child Health Rights, Support Orphans and Vulnerable Children, Promote Nutrition and access to Food supplements, Community interventions against Adolescent Pregnancies and Forced Marriages; and Provide Socio-Economic livelihood support to vulnerable persons; engage communities on environmental protection and conservation of natural resources.

Our Philosophy

  • 1. Identify committed women, men, and youth leaders from among the community for planning and implementing all the development programs.
  • 2. Involve the community in each and every step of the development programs.
  • 3. Facilitating the development of vulnerable communities to attain self- reliance.

Our Team

Meet our amazing Team the one putting effort together and ensuring we achieve all our goals

Mr. Benedict Ngole

CEO/Founder

African Network For Change. Benedict Ngole has tremendous experience in community health education and supporting adolescent friendly space to protect adolescent girls from teenage pregnancy. He is passionate about women's rights, Access to justice and promotion of adolescent and women livelihood.

Benedict has a masters in business administration (MBA), Masters of Arts in Philosophy, BA. Philosophy and Diploma in Social and theological studies.

He is currently works as CEO of African Network for change, linking the organisation to great partners, providing leadership, guidance and management support to the team.

Dr. Susan Nabulobi

Project Coordinator

She is passionate about maternal and child health. Her joy is to support women of reproductive age, promote institutional delivery,fight against traditional birth attendant (TBA), promote child care, immunisation, nutrition and support of orphans and vulnerable children ( OVC). She has supported community health teams, Village health teams (VHTS), Strengthen health facility systems and supported " Mother to Child first" program. Dr. Susan is a consultant ENT surgeon at Lira referral hospital. She holds masters of medicine ENT, surgeon, and Bachelor of medicine and surgery.

Mr. Lameck Olal

Senior Project Associate consultant-Community Health Program

Mr. Lameck is deeply passionate on youth , adolescent girls and young mothers empowerment. Promoted intervention in malaria prevention against children under. 5 years, malaria prevention and control in pregnancy and provide skills to VHTS on malaria smart homes, test and treat, vector control, promoted through indoor residual spraying (IRS), strengthening social marketing in malaria prevention and provide research on treatment and diagnosis of malaria in pregnancy.

Lameck is an accomplished senior clinical Officer supporting Atipe Health Centre III in OYAM district. He is a team player and project mobiliser. He is down to earth with community health leaders and teams.

Ogwang Emmanuel

Project officer

Emmanuel Ogwang is a team player in promoting adolescent friendly spaces in protecting and safeguarding adolescent girls from sexual and gender based violence ( SGBV). He plays critical roles in promoting community sensitisation against violence against women and young girls, support the return to school of adolescent girls who are victims of sexual violence like rape, defilement, forced marriages and teenage pregnancy. He coordinates the promotion of children's rights, promoting schools advocacy in prevention of child marriages, strengthening teachers - children relationship, promote reproductive health programmes in schools, creation of school adolescent health clubs, promote dialogue meetings with community leaders on traditional myths, misconception and norms that hinders girls education. Emanuel ogwang passion is in promotion of sexual health education in schools and community to fight against STIs, teenage pregnancy, forced marriages, children rights and reintegration of adolescent boys and girls back to school, provision of livelihood support to teenage mothers and alternative education to victims of SGBV.

Isaac Newton Obua

Senior Project Officer

Isaac has a diploma in clinical medicine.
