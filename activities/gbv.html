A Journey Through Darkness

  • Apio Sarah
  • Oyam District

Sarah’s life, like that of many women, was deeply affected by the invisible chains of gender-based violence. For years, she endured emotional, physical, and psychological abuse in a relationship she once believed was built on love and trust. The abuse chipped away at her sense of self-worth, leaving her feeling trapped, powerless, and isolated.

In Uganda, as in many parts of the world, social and cultural norms often silence victims of GBV. Fear of stigma, retaliation, and shame prevent many women from seeking help, leaving them vulnerable to continued violence.

Finding the Courage to Break Free

Sarah’s turning point came one fateful evening when the violence escalated to a level she could no longer endure. Driven by a newfound sense of survival and determination, she made the courageous decision to leave. With the help of a local women’s support group and a friend she confided in, Sarah found refuge in a shelter that provided counseling, legal advice, and a safe space to heal.

“It was terrifying at first,” Sarah admits. “I had no idea what the future held, but for the first time in years, I felt a sense of relief. I wasn’t alone anymore after getting into contact with AN4CHANGE.”

The African Network for Change as a shelter connected Sarah with resources that helped her rebuild her life. She participated in counseling sessions that addressed the trauma she had experienced, and she began to reconnect with her inner strength. Slowly, she started to rediscover the confident, capable woman she had once been.

Empowering Others: A New Mission

As Sarah regained her independence, she knew her story wasn’t over. Having experienced firsthand the devastating impact of gender-based violence, she became passionate about helping other women escape similar situations. She started volunteering at the same shelter that had saved her, sharing her story and offering support to other survivors.

“The moment I started sharing my experience, I realized how many other women felt the same way I once did—trapped, alone, and voiceless. It’s heartbreaking, but it also motivated me to help them see that there is a way out,” Sarah explains.

In time, Sarah became a vocal advocate for gender equality and women’s rights. She began working with local NGOs and community leaders to raise awareness about GBV, hosting workshops and speaking at public events. Her efforts have reached hundreds of women and girls, providing them with the tools, resources, and confidence they need to reclaim their lives.

Sarah Together with team from African Network for Change

A Voice for Change

Today, Sarah is a prominent advocate in her community, using her voice to demand better legal protections for victims of gender-based violence. Her advocacy work extends beyond raising awareness; she actively campaigns for stronger enforcement of laws against GBV, better access to support services, and initiatives to educate men and boys on gender equality.

“Ending gender-based violence isn’t just about helping women leave abusive situations—it’s about changing the way society views women,” Sarah says. “We need to create environments where women are safe, respected, and valued, and where violence is never tolerated.”

Through her unwavering dedication, Sarah has become a role model not only for survivors of GBV but also for communities that are learning to confront this issue head-on. Her work is helping to shift attitudes and challenge harmful norms that perpetuate violence against women.

Inspiring Others to Rise

Sarah’s journey from survivor to advocate is a testament to the strength of the human spirit. Her story is a reminder that while gender-based violence remains a harsh reality for many, there is hope for change. She has shown that with support, courage, and determination, it is possible to overcome even the darkest of circumstances.

“Surviving is just the first step,” Sarah reflects. “The real victory comes when you reclaim your life and use your experience to uplift others. I’m proof that it’s possible to not only survive but to thrive.”

Sarah’s story is one of resilience, courage, and empowerment. It shows us that even in the face of extreme adversity, women have the power to rise, rebuild, and lead the fight to end gender-based violence. Through her advocacy, she is helping to create a world where every woman can live free from fear, violence, and oppression—a world where women’s voices are heard, and their rights are protected.

Take Action

Sarah’s story is a call to action for all of us. Whether you’re a survivor, an ally, or simply someone who cares about equality, you can make a difference. Stand up against gender-based violence, support survivors, and advocate for policies that protect women and girls. Together, we can create a safer, more just world for all.
